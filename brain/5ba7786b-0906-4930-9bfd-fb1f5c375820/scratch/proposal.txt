Social Products — Company & Product Intelligence
A Social Listening and Product Intelligence Module for Competitive Market Insights
1.  Executive Summary
In a hyper-competitive market landscape, businesses that understand how customers perceive their products — and how they compare with rivals — hold a decisive strategic advantage. Social media platforms, app stores, and review communities generate millions of data points every day that capture authentic public sentiment about companies and products. Yet most 
organisations
 lack the infrastructure to systematically harvest and interpret these signals at scale.
This project proposes the design and implementation of a Social Products — Company & Product Intelligence module: a social listening and product intelligence platform that aggregates company-level and product-level signals from social platforms, review sources, and community forums. The system will produce ranked competitive intelligence dashboards, sentiment-scored product reports, and real-time alert feeds, enabling stakeholders to make informed decisions about product positioning, marketing, and feature 
development.
2.  Problem Statement
Consumer opinions are scattered across dozens of digital touchpoints — Twitter/X, Reddit, LinkedIn, Google Play, App Store, Amazon, G2, Trustpilot, and niche forums. The volume, velocity, and variety of this unstructured text data far 
exceeds
 what any manual team can process. As a result, 
organisations
 miss emerging product issues, fail to track competitive repositioning in real time, and react to market shifts only after damage has been done.
Specific pain points this project addresses:
No unified view of brand health across multiple social and review channels
Inability to compare product perception against direct competitors in real time
Trending feature requests and pain points buried within thousands of unstructured reviews
Delayed response to sudden spikes in negative sentiment — reputational damage compounds
Manual analyst effort required to compile weekly competitive intelligence reports
This framework directly addresses the gap by automating the aggregation, analysis, scoring, and 
visualisation
 of company and product intelligence from the social web.
3.  Project Objectives
Monitor and collect social media mentions of target companies and their products across major platforms.
Aggregate review signals from app stores, e-commerce platforms, and SaaS review sites.
Apply Natural Language Processing (NLP) to compute sentiment scores at company and product levels.
Rank companies and products by sentiment score, mention volume, and engagement metrics.
Identify trending features, pain points, and innovation opportunities from product reviews.
Benchmark product perception against direct competitors using comparative analytics.
Deliver a real-time alerting system for sudden sentiment shifts or viral mentions.
Produce a weekly aggregated intelligence dashboard and automated report for stakeholders.

5.  Key Components and System Architecture
5.1  Module
 A — Social Product Monitoring
The monitoring sub-module continuously ingests social media mentions and maps them to specific companies and products. It uses keyword and entity matching against a configurable watchlist, then channels the data into the central analytics pipeline.
Mention Collection: 
Scheduled crawlers and API connectors fetch posts, tweets, and threads matching company or product keywords
Review Aggregation: 
Scraping and API adapters pull structured ratings and text reviews from app stores and review platforms
Volume Tracking: 
Time-series counts of mentions and reviews per entity per platform — used for trend analysis and anomaly detection
Company & Product Ranking: 
Entities scored and ranked by weighted composite of sentiment score and mention volume
5.2  Module
 B — Product Review Analysis
The review analysis sub-module processes collected review text through an NLP pipeline to extract granular insights about product perception.
Sentiment Classification: 
Pre-trained transformer models (BERT / 
RoBERTa
) classify each review as positive, negative, or neutral
Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis: 
Fine-grained extraction of opinions about specific product features (UI, performance, pricing, support)
Trending Feature Detection: 
TF-IDF and LDA topic modelling identify frequently mentioned product attributes over rolling time windows
Pain Point Identification: 
Negative review clustering reveals recurring issues, enabling product teams to 
prioritise
 fixes
Competitor Benchmarking: 
Side-by-side sentiment score comparison and feature gap analysis against rival products
5.3  Intelligence
 Dashboard and Alerts
A web-based dashboard consolidates all analytical outputs into actionable intelligence views. The alert engine monitors for threshold breaches and dispatches notifications in real time.
